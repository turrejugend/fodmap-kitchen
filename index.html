What is FODMAP?

The diet that actually helps IBS

FODMAPs are a group of short-chain carbohydrates that are poorly absorbed in the gut. For people with IBS, they can cause bloating, cramps, and other symptoms.

The low-FODMAP diet — developed by Monash University — helps identify and manage trigger foods. It's clinically proven to reduce IBS symptoms in up to 75% of people who try it.
