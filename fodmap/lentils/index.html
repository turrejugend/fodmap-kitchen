Lentils

⚠️
Caution — Canned only, small portion
Canned lentils (drained and rinsed) are low FODMAP at ½ cup (46g). Dried, cooked lentils are high FODMAP at the same serving size. The canning process leaches out some GOS.

Why are canned lentils safer than cooked from scratch?

Lentils are high in GOS (galacto-oligosaccharides) — the legume FODMAP. However, when lentils are canned, a significant portion of the GOS leaches into the canning liquid. Draining and rinsing removes much of this liquid, substantially reducing the FODMAP content compared to freshly cooked lentils.

Monash University certifies ½ cup (46g) of canned, drained, rinsed lentils as low FODMAP. The same amount of freshly cooked lentils is high FODMAP. This distinction matters enormously in practice.

Tips & alternatives

  • 🥫
    Always use canned, always rinse
    Drain the can completely, then rinse under cold water for 30 seconds. This removes the FODMAP-rich liquid.
  • 🫘
    Firm tofu
    Low FODMAP, high protein, and reliable. Marinate and bake for a lentil substitute in salads and grain bowls.
  • 🥚
    Eggs
    The simplest high-protein, zero-FODMAP option for meals that traditionally use lentils.

Frequently asked questions

Is lentil soup low FODMAP?

Usually not. Most lentil soups use large amounts of lentils plus garlic and onion. A low-FODMAP version would use canned lentils (½ cup maximum), garlic-infused oil, and spring onion greens — and would be much lighter than a traditional lentil soup.

Are red lentils lower FODMAP than green?

Both are high FODMAP when cooked from dry. When canned, both can be low FODMAP at the same ½ cup portion. Colour doesn't determine FODMAP content.
