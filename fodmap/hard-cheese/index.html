Hard Cheese

✅
Low FODMAP — Safe
Hard aged cheeses (cheddar, parmesan, pecorino, gruyère) are low FODMAP. The aging process removes virtually all lactose, leaving behind mostly protein and fat.

Why is hard cheese low FODMAP when milk isn't?

During cheese-making, bacteria convert lactose into lactic acid. In hard, aged cheeses, this process is nearly complete — by the time you eat parmesan or cheddar, virtually no lactose remains. This makes them low FODMAP despite being dairy products.

The rule of thumb: the harder and more aged the cheese, the lower the lactose, and the safer for IBS. Soft, fresh cheeses (ricotta, cream cheese, cottage cheese) retain most of their lactose and are high FODMAP. Mozzarella falls in between — low FODMAP at 40g.

Tips & alternatives

  • 🧀
    Use parmesan and pecorino generously
    Both are zero-FODMAP for practical purposes. Grate freely over pasta, soups, and salads. These are the backbone of carbonara and many Italian dishes.
  • ✅
    Safe hard cheeses
    Cheddar, parmesan, pecorino, gruyère, gouda, brie, and camembert are all low FODMAP. Avoid cream cheese, ricotta, and cottage cheese.

Frequently asked questions

Is mozzarella low FODMAP?

Yes, in portions up to 40g (about 1.5 oz). Fresh mozzarella has slightly more lactose than hard aged cheeses but is still low FODMAP at moderate amounts.

Is brie low FODMAP?

Yes. Brie and camembert are low FODMAP at 2 slices (40g). Despite being soft, they are aged long enough to have very low lactose content.
