Oat milk

⚠️
Caution — Check the label

Oat milk can be low or high FODMAP depending on the brand and serving size. Plain oat milk in small amounts (30ml) may be tolerated, but most commercial oat milks contain enough fructans to trigger symptoms in larger quantities. Lactose-free dairy milk or almond milk are safer daily alternatives.

Why is oat milk tricky on a low-FODMAP diet?

Safe portion: Up to 30ml if tolerated. A full glass or large coffee is likely to be high FODMAP.

Oats themselves contain fructans, and the milling and blending process used to make oat milk releases these into the liquid. The FODMAP content varies significantly between brands.

Monash University testing indicates that 30ml of oat milk may be tolerated, but a standard glass (200–250ml) is likely high FODMAP. Most people use oat milk in larger quantities — in coffee, cereal, or smoothies — which is where the problem lies.

If you want to use a milk alternative freely, lactose-free dairy milk, rice milk, or macadamia milk are safer low-FODMAP choices with no portion anxiety.

Tips & alternatives

  • 🥛
    Lactose-free dairy milk
    The easiest swap. Same taste and texture, no FODMAPs. Works in coffee, cereal, baking, and cooking.
  • 🌾
    Rice milk
    Low FODMAP in standard servings. Slightly thinner than oat milk. Good for cereal and cooking.
  • 🌰
    Macadamia milk
    Low FODMAP and creamy — closest texture to oat milk for coffee drinks.
