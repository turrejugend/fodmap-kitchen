Eggs

✅
Low FODMAP — Safe

Eggs are completely FODMAP-free. They contain no fermentable carbohydrates and are one of the safest, most versatile proteins on the low-FODMAP diet. Enjoy them freely in any quantity and preparation.

Why are eggs safe on a low-FODMAP diet?

FODMAPs are a specific type of fermentable carbohydrate. Eggs contain virtually no carbohydrates at all — they are made up of protein and fat, with no FODMAPs present.

This makes eggs one of the most reliable ingredients for people with IBS. Whether scrambled, poached, fried, boiled, or baked, eggs will not contribute to FODMAP load.

The only thing to watch with egg dishes is what you cook them with — garlic, onion, or wheat-based accompaniments would make an egg dish problematic. The eggs themselves are always safe.

Tips & alternatives

  • 🍳
    No alternatives needed
    Eggs are already FODMAP-free. Enjoy them in any quantity. Just be mindful of other ingredients in egg dishes — garlic, onion, and regular bread are the common culprits.
  • 👩‍🍳
    Cook with garlic-infused oil
    To make scrambled eggs or omelettes flavourful without FODMAPs, use garlic-infused oil instead of butter with garlic.
