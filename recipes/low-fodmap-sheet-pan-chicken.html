Sheet-Pan Chicken & Garlic Yogurt

Crispy thighs over roasted potatoes with a creamy lactose-free garlic yogurt sauce. One pan, 40 minutes.

Prep10 min
Cook40 min
Serves2–3
MealDinner
FODMAP status✓ FODMAP safe
The FODMAP swaps
→
Regular yogurt
Lactose-free Greek yogurt
Standard yogurt retains significant lactose. Lactose-free yogurt is identical in texture and tang — the perfect creamy sauce base.
→
Garlic cloves
Garlic-infused olive oil
Garlic is high FODMAP at any amount. Garlic-infused oil carries all the flavour because fructans are not oil-soluble.

Ingredients

  • Bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs 4
  • Baby potatoes, halved 500g
  • Garlic-infused olive oil Safe swap for garlic ↗4 tbsp
  • Smoked paprika 1 tsp
  • Salt and black pepper To taste
  • Lactose-free Greek yogurt Safe swap ↗200g
  • Lemon, juiced ½
  • Fresh dill Small bunch
  • Rocket / arugula 2 handfuls
  • Bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs 4
  • Baby potatoes, halved 1 lb
  • Garlic-infused olive oil Safe swap for garlic ↗4 tbsp
  • Smoked paprika 1 tsp
  • Salt and black pepper To taste
  • Lactose-free Greek yogurt Safe swap ↗¾ cup
  • Lemon, juiced ½
  • Fresh dill Small bunch
  • Rocket / arugula 2 handfuls

Method

  1. 1
    Preheat and prep potatoes

    Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F). Toss halved baby potatoes with 2 tbsp garlic-infused oil, a generous pinch of salt, and black pepper on a large sheet pan. Spread in a single layer.

  2. 2
    Season and arrange chicken

    Pat chicken thighs dry with paper towels — this is what gets the skin crispy. Rub all over with remaining garlic-infused oil, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Place skin-side up directly on top of the potatoes.

  3. 3
    Roast until golden

    Roast for 35–40 minutes until the chicken skin is deep golden and crispy, and a thermometer reads 74°C (165°F) at the thickest part. The potatoes underneath will absorb the chicken juices and caramelise at the edges.

  4. 4
    Make the yogurt sauce

    While the chicken roasts, combine the lactose-free yogurt, 1 tbsp garlic-infused oil, lemon juice, chopped dill, and a pinch of salt. Taste and adjust — it should be tangy and herby.

  5. 5
    Plate and serve

    Spread yogurt sauce on the base of the plate or serving dish. Pile on the rocket, then the potatoes and chicken. Finish with a little more dill and a drizzle of oil.

Key tip: Pat the chicken skin completely dry before roasting. Any surface moisture turns to steam in the oven, which prevents browning. Dry skin = crispy skin.
