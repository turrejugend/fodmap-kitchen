No-Knead Bread

Five minutes of effort, overnight patience. Spelt flour with long fermentation makes this crusty loaf low-FODMAP.

Prep5 min + overnight
Cook45 min
Serves1 loaf (12 slices)
MealSides
FODMAP status⚠ Watch portions
The FODMAP swaps
→
All-purpose / strong white flour
White spelt flour
Regular wheat flour is high in fructans. White spelt flour has lower fructan content, and crucially, the long fermentation process breaks down much of it further.
→
Short rise (2–4 hours)
Long cold fermentation (12–18 hours)
Time is the key swap. Extended fermentation allows bacteria to pre-digest the fructans in the flour — the same principle that makes sourdough lower FODMAP. Don't rush it.

Ingredients

  • White spelt flour Low-FODMAP swap ↗400g
  • Fine salt 1¼ tsp
  • Instant yeast ¼ tsp
  • Warm water (about 20°C) 320ml
  • White spelt flour Low-FODMAP swap ↗3 cups
  • Fine salt 1¼ tsp
  • Instant yeast ¼ tsp
  • Warm water (about 70°F) 1⅓ cups

Method

  1. 1
    Mix (5 minutes)

    In a large bowl, whisk together spelt flour, salt, and yeast. Add water and stir with a wooden spoon or your hand until a shaggy dough forms — no dry patches. It will look rough and sticky. Cover tightly with clingfilm or a plate.

  2. 2
    Long cold rise (12–18 hours)

    Leave at room temperature for 12–18 hours. The dough is ready when it has roughly doubled and the surface is dotted with bubbles. The longer end produces more flavour and better fructan breakdown — don't rush it.

  3. 3
    Shape

    Turn the dough onto a well-floured surface. It will be sticky — use a dough scraper if you have one. Fold it over itself a few times to form a rough ball. Place seam-side down on a floured tea towel or in a flour-dusted proving basket. Cover and rest for 2 hours.

  4. 4
    Preheat the pot

    30 minutes before baking, place a lidded Dutch oven (cast iron or heavy oven-safe pot) in the oven and heat to 240°C (475°F). The pot must be screaming hot — this is what creates the crust.

  5. 5
    Bake covered, then uncovered

    Carefully lower the dough into the hot pot (use baking paper as a sling if needed). Cover and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the lid and bake a further 12–15 minutes until the crust is a deep mahogany brown. Cool on a wire rack for at least 30 minutes before cutting.

Portion note: Low-FODMAP serving is 2 slices per sitting. Spelt still contains some fructans — the fermentation reduces them significantly but doesn't eliminate them. Stick to 2 slices and enjoy. The loaf freezes well sliced.
