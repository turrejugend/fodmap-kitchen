Original Plum Torte

The most requested recipe in NYT Cooking history — a buttery spelt cake with jammy caramelised plums.

Prep15 min
Cook50 min
Serves8 slices
MealDessert
FODMAP status⚠ Watch portions

White spelt flour
Lower in fructans than regular wheat flour. The short baking time means less fermentation than bread, so stick to 1 slice per sitting.

Plums contain sorbitol — a polyol FODMAP. One plum per serving is the Monash-certified safe amount. Don't add extra plums, and keep your slice to one.

Ingredients

  • White spelt flour Low-FODMAP swap ↗150g
  • Caster sugar 150g
  • Unsalted butter, softened FODMAP-free ↗115g
  • Eggs FODMAP-free ↗2 large
  • Baking powder 1 tsp
  • Pinch of salt
  • Ripe plums, halved and stoned Max 8 — watch portions ↗8
  • Caster sugar (for topping) 2 tsp
  • Lemon juice 1 tsp
  • Cinnamon (optional) ½ tsp
  • White spelt flour Low-FODMAP swap ↗1¼ cups
  • Caster / granulated sugar ¾ cup
  • Unsalted butter, softened FODMAP-free ↗½ cup (1 stick)
  • Eggs FODMAP-free ↗2 large
  • Baking powder 1 tsp
  • Pinch of salt
  • Ripe plums, halved and stoned Max 8 — watch portions ↗8
  • Sugar (for topping) 2 tsp
  • Lemon juice 1 tsp
  • Cinnamon (optional) ½ tsp

Method

  1. 1
    Preheat and prep

    Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F). Butter a 23cm (9-inch) springform tin and line the base with baking paper.

  2. 2
    Cream butter and sugar

    Beat the softened butter and sugar together until pale and fluffy — about 3 minutes with a hand mixer, or 5 minutes by hand. The mixture should look almost white and noticeably increased in volume.

  3. 3
    Add eggs and dry ingredients

    Beat in the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each. Sift in the spelt flour, baking powder, and salt. Fold gently until just combined — don't overmix.

  4. 4
    Add plums and bake

    Spread the batter into the prepared tin. Press the plum halves cut-side up into the batter in a single layer — they will sink slightly as they bake. Sprinkle with sugar, lemon juice, and cinnamon if using.

  5. 5
    Bake until golden

    Bake for 45–55 minutes until the cake is golden brown, springs back lightly when touched, and a skewer inserted in the centre comes out clean. Cool in the tin for 10 minutes before releasing the springform.

Portion note: 1 slice = 1 plum. Plums contain sorbitol, and the safe low-FODMAP serving is 1 plum per sitting. Slice the torte into 8 and stop at one. The cake also freezes beautifully by the slice — wrap individually in clingfilm.
