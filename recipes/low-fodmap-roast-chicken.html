Buttermilk-Brined Roast Chicken

Overnight brining with lactose-free buttermilk. Impossibly juicy, golden skin that shatters.

Prep15 min
Cook1 hr 15 min
Serves4
MealDinner
FODMAP status✓ FODMAP safe
The FODMAP swaps
→
Regular buttermilk
Lactose-free buttermilk (or thinned lactose-free yogurt)
Regular buttermilk contains lactose. Lactose-free versions are otherwise identical — same acidity, same tenderising effect on the chicken.
→
Garlic cloves in the brine
Garlic-infused olive oil
Garlic fructans are water-soluble, so they'd leach into the brine and coat the chicken. Garlic-infused oil gives the same flavour safely.

Ingredients

  • Whole chicken 1.5–2kg
  • Lactose-free buttermilk Safe swap ↗500ml
  • Garlic-infused olive oil Safe swap for garlic ↗3 tbsp
  • Fine salt 2 tsp
  • Black pepper 1 tsp
  • Lemon, halved 1
  • Fresh thyme + rosemary Few sprigs
  • Whole chicken 3.5–4.5 lb
  • Lactose-free buttermilk Safe swap ↗2 cups
  • Garlic-infused olive oil Safe swap for garlic ↗3 tbsp
  • Fine salt 2 tsp
  • Black pepper 1 tsp
  • Lemon, halved 1
  • Fresh thyme + rosemary Few sprigs

Method

  1. 1
    Brine overnight

    Combine lactose-free buttermilk, salt, and pepper in a large zip-lock bag or container. Add the chicken, seal, and refrigerate overnight — 12 hours minimum, 24 hours maximum.

  2. 2
    Bring to room temperature

    Remove the chicken from the brine and pat completely dry with paper towels. This step matters — wet skin steams instead of crisps. Let it sit at room temperature for 30–45 minutes. Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F).

  3. 3
    Season and roast

    Rub the chicken all over with garlic-infused oil. Stuff the cavity with lemon halves and herb sprigs. Place breast-side up on a rack in a roasting tin. Roast at 220°C for 15 minutes until the skin starts to colour.

  4. 4
    Finish at lower heat

    Reduce to 190°C (375°F) and continue roasting for 50–60 minutes, until the juices run clear when you pierce the thigh joint, and an internal thermometer reads 74°C (165°F) at the thickest part of the thigh.

  5. 5
    Rest before carving

    Transfer to a board and rest uncovered for 10–15 minutes. Non-negotiable — this is what makes it juicy.

Key tip: The brine is what separates this from every other roast chicken. The lactic acid denatures the proteins slightly, allowing the meat to hold more moisture during cooking. Don't skip it or shorten it.
