What is the FODMAP diet?

The low FODMAP diet is a clinically proven approach to managing irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), developed by researchers at Monash University in Australia. FODMAP stands for Fermentable Oligosaccharides, Disaccharides, Monosaccharides and Polyols — a group of short-chain carbohydrates that are poorly absorbed in the small intestine.

When unabsorbed FODMAPs reach the large intestine, they are fermented by gut bacteria, producing gas and drawing water into the bowel. For people with IBS, this triggers symptoms including bloating, abdominal pain, cramping, diarrhoea and constipation.

Studies show the low FODMAP diet reduces IBS symptoms in up to 75% of people who follow it, making it one of the most effective dietary interventions for IBS available.

Common FODMAP mistakes to avoid

Using garlic and onion powder

Many people switch from fresh garlic and onion to powders thinking it's safer. It isn't — garlic and onion powder are highly concentrated in fructans and can trigger symptoms even in tiny amounts hidden in spice blends. Use garlic-infused oil instead, which gives the flavour without the FODMAPs.

Ignoring portion sizes

FODMAP content is dose-dependent. Some foods like rolled oats, avocado and broccoli are safe in small portions but trigger symptoms in larger amounts. Always pay attention to serving sizes, especially for caution-rated foods.

Assuming "gluten-free" means FODMAP-safe

Gluten-free and low FODMAP are not the same thing. Many gluten-free products contain high-FODMAP ingredients like apple juice, honey or inulin. Always check the full ingredient list.

Not rinsing canned legumes

Canned chickpeas and lentils can be low FODMAP in small portions — but only if thoroughly rinsed. The canning liquid contains FODMAPs that leach from the legumes. Drain and rinse well before using.

Frequently asked questions

How long does the FODMAP elimination phase last?
The elimination phase typically lasts 2–6 weeks, during which all high-FODMAP foods are avoided. After that, foods are reintroduced one at a time to identify personal triggers. Always do this with guidance from a registered dietitian if possible.
Can I eat out on a FODMAP diet?
Yes, with some planning. Safe options at most restaurants include grilled meat or fish with rice or plain potatoes, salads without dressings containing garlic or onion, and sushi (with tamari instead of regular soy sauce). Always ask about ingredients when in doubt.
Is garlic-infused oil really FODMAP safe?
Yes — FODMAPs are water soluble, not oil soluble. This means the fructans in garlic don't transfer into the oil during infusion. Commercially prepared garlic-infused oil is safe, as is homemade as long as no garlic pieces remain in the oil.
What's the difference between FODMAP safe and FODMAP free?
FODMAP safe means a food contains FODMAPs at low enough levels that most people with IBS can tolerate a normal serving without symptoms. FODMAP free means a food contains no measurable FODMAPs. Most foods labelled as safe still contain trace amounts.
Do I have to follow the FODMAP diet forever?
No. The low FODMAP diet is a diagnostic tool, not a permanent lifestyle. After the elimination and reintroduction phases, most people find they can tolerate many foods in normal portions — they just need to avoid or limit their specific triggers.
